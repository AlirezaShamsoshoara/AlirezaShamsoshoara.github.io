## [AI Daily Paper Summary](/blog/ai-papers/)

I read AI and ML research papers daily and post concise summaries here. Each entry covers the key contributions, methods, and takeaways from recent papers across topics like deep learning, reinforcement learning, NLP, computer vision, and more.

<!-- LATEST_PAPERS_START -->
**Latest — July 15, 2026**

1. **PAST-TIDE** — Reframes stance detection as cloze-style masked language modeling with prototype-anchored contrastive learning, scoring 0.75/0.74 macro-F1 on cross-topic Arabic stance detection. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.04690](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04690)
2. **Multi-Agent LLMs Fail to Explore Each Other** — Shows LLM agents exhibit myopic, polarized behavior instead of effective exploration when interacting, and proposes MACE to fix peer-selection dynamics. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.11250](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11250)
3. **MuScriptor** — An open-weight multi-instrument music transcription model combining synthetic pretraining, real-audio fine-tuning, and RL post-training. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.08168](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08168)
4. **Read It Back (SpectraReward)** — Turns pretrained MLLMs into training-free, zero-shot reward models for text-to-image RL by measuring prompt-reconstruction fidelity. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.11886](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11886)
5. **Evidence-Backed Video Question Answering** — Requires video LLMs to output spatio-temporal evidence alongside answers, exposing a gap between QA accuracy and true visual grounding. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.11862](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11862)
6. **Principled Analysis of Deep RL Evaluation** — Argues canonical RL benchmarking paradigms produce non-monotonic, misleading performance rankings across data regimes. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.07769](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07769)
7. **Blind-Spots-Bench** — A "trivial-for-humans" benchmark exposing multimodal model failures, finding closed-source models can lead open-weight ones by ~10 points. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.08317](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08317)
8. **Search Beyond What Can Be Taught** — Introduces SearchGen-Bench, where frontier visual generators score only 21–28/100 on knowledge-boundary tasks, plus a "teach-then-search" fix. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.05382](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05382)
9. **SynthDocBench** — A factor-controlled synthetic benchmark isolating causes of long-context visual document understanding failures in frontier VLMs. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.10400](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10400)
10. **AMID** — An autonomous multi-agent framework automating medical imaging model development, matching strong human-designed challenge solutions on several tasks. [arxiv.org/abs/2607.10522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10522)
